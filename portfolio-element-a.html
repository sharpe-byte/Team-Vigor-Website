Element A: Problem Justification

Problem Statement

Since our devices where use on a daily basics are such susceptible to damage, we will find a way to inherently improve the durability and life span of these overused devices.

Artifact Summary: The article begins with saying that the best smartphones currently in the market offer “extreme durability”, massive batteries and specific features to outdoor cases. Normally these smartphones are dust and water resistant, or sometimes totally water resistant. The articles still continues to state some features these “rugged” smartphones has. The article then ends with their recommendations for phones.

Artifact Critique: The article comes off as to say that phones are already durable and need little to no room for improvement. Even in the need of the user not liking the durability of the phones, they have option to special case specific features. The articles refers to phone durability as if they're already too durable in higher end models and the problem is slowly fading.

They continue to state reasons that they phones are being dumped, display, back cover, and software to a few of many continuous factors. They state that the problem can be easily be fixed by making more durable phones, the article continues giving data to manufactures that might consider making more durable phones.

Artifact Critique: This article clearly states the phones are not durable enough, it even reaches to say that that the software used in these smartphones is not enough with the average life expectancy of a phone to be about two years. They support our claim that phone need to become more durable, because in the case of simply making more durable phone can lead from the environmental issues of these deteriorating phones.

Article #4

Source (APA format): Nield, D. (n.d.). We Just Got Closer to Smartphone Screens That Can 'Heal' Their Own Cracks. Retrieved from https://www.sciencealert.com/we-re-another-step-towards-smartphone-screens-that-can-heal-themselves

Artifact Summary: This article shows that a group in the South Korean institute of science and technology developed a “self-healing electronic material” that repairs itself over time including scratches, cracks, and other damage that can be caused to the phone from drops. The material is based on linseed oil, a oil made from flax plant seeds, the material is still too new to begin being used in phones as of now but this shows that there are still advancements that can be made in phone durability.

Artifact Critique: As seen in the article, future phones could include self-healing screens and material that could make things like cases and screen protectors absolute. Because of the simple ingredient of Flax plant seed oil this technology can be easily adapted to work with newer displays being made like flexible and folding displays that are being made by Samsung and Motorola.

Professional Opinions

Professional #1: Cellular Repair on JFK

  • Do you believe that the current durability of phones is a problem that needs to be addressed?

    "I do believe that they are not as durable as they used to be. It's more so for looks than anything else with all the glass. They best thing they come with is water resistance. Even though it's very susceptible to change."

  • What is the number one reason people come into your store? What caused their phone to break?

    "The main reasons for people coming in are broken screens and either dirty or broken charging ports."

  • How many people who came into your store had a case on their phone when the phone was broken?

    "I would say about from half to about 60% of people."

  • Have more people had damage to the screen of the phone or the back of the phone?

    "It depends, altogether though it's the screen. A significant amount of Samsung customers do damage to the screen of their phone. The back glass hold up better with Samsungs. With Iphones it's a 60/40 split."
